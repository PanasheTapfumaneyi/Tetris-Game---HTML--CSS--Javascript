Description

Russian game inventor Alexey Pajitnov invented the renowned puzzle video game Tetris in 1984. The objective of the game is to create uninterrupted horizontal lines by manipulating "tetrominoes," which are falling geometric forms. A gapless created line vanishes, making room for more formations. The falling forms get faster as the game goes on, testing the player's reflexes and quick thinking. The main objective is to keep tetrominoes from filling the screen. Tetris is widely known for its easy accessibility, captivating gameplay, and difficult level, making it one of the most well-known and classic video games ever created.
